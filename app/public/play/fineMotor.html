Thread

thread /THred/: assemble small objects together or singly on a thread, chain, or skewer

1.Create your structure to thread. Here are some ideas:

Spaghetti stabbed into playdough, color string, pipe cleaners, crazy straws, rope, wire

2.Choose your material to thread with. Some ideas include:

Cheerios or other O-shaped cereal (Fruit Loops), beads, pool noodles, buttons, paper tubes, bird food, any paper product punctured with a hole (egg carton), twizzlers, leaves, pasta

3.Combine these in any way you or your child desires! As stated you can puncture a lot of things with holes and thread them. For older children use dull needles to guide their structure through their threading material. Have fun!

Weave

weave /wēv/: form (fabric or a fabric item) by interlacing long threads passing in one direction with others at a right angle to them

1. You fill first need something that can act as a “loom.” A loom is any structure that allows the child to weave their thread back and forth through. Here are some ideas for looms:

Plastic sink mat with holes, wired basket, wooden dish drying rack, baby gates, fencing, homemade structure using straight material repeatedly

2. Once your structure is built or found use any material to guide through the holes/posts. Here are my suggestions but the options are limitless:

Grass helms, leaves, flower stems, rope/string, ribbon/party banner ribbon, tinsel, rubber bands, paper strips, pipe cleaners, feathers, straw
