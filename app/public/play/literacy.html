Letter Fishing

letter /ˈledər/: a character representing one or more of the sounds used in speech; any of the symbols of an alphabet

1. Gather Your Materials

You will need a large container, plastic or metal alphabet letters, strainers, and blue food dye.

2. Set Up the Play Area

Fill the large container with water and a small amount of blue food dye.

3. Play

Children can fish out different letters, guess the letter name, sort letters, and sort by color.

Mailbox Words

word /wərd/: a single distinct meaningful element of speech or writing

1. Cut Out Your Mailbox Shape

Cut out the shape by drawing an elongated rectangle, and fold approximately 1/4 of the shape.

2. Create the Dome Shape

Join the Two short ends and glue it together. Now you have your basic mailbox shape! Write your word family on the outside.

3. Play

Set out your mailboxes so the kids can read them. Set out your stack of envelopes. Tell the child to deliver the "notes" to the family they belong to.

Alphabet Blocks

alphabet /ˈalfəˌbet/: a set of letters or symbols in a fixed order, used to represent the basic sounds of a language

1. Create the Alphabet Blocks

Write out the alphabet once using upper-case letters and once using lower-case letters on Duplos using a non-permanent marker.

2. Play

Children can build, they can spell, make an alphabet tower, and they can match up the upper and lowercase letters.
