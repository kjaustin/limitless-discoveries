Sense

sense /sens/: a faculty by which the body perceives an external stimulus; one of the faculties of sight, smell, hearing, taste, and touch

Sensory Walking Path

Sensory experiences are an important part of child development, but many sensory activities only utilize the hands. Our feet are just as sensitive as our hands and this activity allows children the experience of learning, exploring, and feeling with their feet.

Recommended items for your sensory path:

Textured pillow, Lego base plate, heating pad, wet newspaper, dried beans, bubble wrap, dried cereal or rice, wooden beam or board, various paint colors, cooked spaghetti noodles

Wheel

wheel /(h)wēl/: to perform (a movement) in a circular or curving direction

1. Place different colored paints in flat trays.

Primary paint colors work the best as mixing them doesn't result in a brown goopy mess, but feel free to use any paints you have at home! You can also add dish soap to water the paints down (also makes cleanup easier) or add cornstarch for a more sidewalk chalk look and feel.

2. Choose your trucks, and material.

If you don't want a huge mess and clearer painted tracks then definitely use large poster sheets as the backdrop, however simply rolling the trucks and cars on the sidewalk is a lot of fun as well.

3. Paint!
