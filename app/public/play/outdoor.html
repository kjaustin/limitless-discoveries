Garden

butterfly /ˈbədərˌflī/: any of numerous diurnal insects of the order Lepidoptera

1. Find a Home

Begin by finding a garden location that receives at least six to eight hours of sunlight and is sheltered from the wind. It also needs to provide nighttime hiding places for butterflies such as small trees or large shrubs, and places for them to attach while in their chrysalis.

2. Choose your Plants

Select plants that grow well in your area. You need to include flowering plants that attract butterflies and also leafy plants that attract egg-laying butterflies and provide food for the caterpillars.

3. Assemble your Garden

Plant your garden. Add one or two large, flat rocks in the sun. Since butterflies cannot drink from open water, provide them with a "puddle" by filling a container with wet sand.
