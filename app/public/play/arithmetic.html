Flower Dice

roll /rōl/: move or cause to move in a particular direction by turning over and over on an axis

1. Gather Materials:

Flower Dice Game Printable, 2 Dice, Material to act as flower petals (Pasta, Twizzlers, Sticks, Petals, Leaves)

2. Roll the dice to see how many flower petals to place around the flower. Roll again and again to learn to identify numbers, count, and add!

Fraction Connect 4

fraction /ˈfrakSH(ə)n/: a numerical quantity that is not a whole number

1. Materials Needed:

Connect Four game, masking tape or small labels, and marker

2. Cut masking tape to fit on game pieces.

Use one colored coins for just 1/4. For older children you can add in a few 1/2, 3/4, and 1/8 on the same color. Use the other colored coins for 1/3. Again, for older children you can add in a few 2/3 and 1/6 on the same color.

3. Start playing!

The goal of the game is to make a whole by lining up four quarters or three thirds of the same color in a row.

Ten Apples Up On Top Counting

count /kount/: determine the total number of (a collection of items)

1. Materials you will need:

10 Apples Up on Top Printable, A paint stick, 11 clothespins, A picture of your child, Scissors, Glue, Card stock (optional)

2. Assemble the activity by glueing the apples and the photo of your child onto the clothespins.
3. Let your child stack the numbers on top of eachother on the paintstick.

For older children, ask them to put them in correct order from smallest to largest. For younger children, don't worry about the order. Practice counting, identifying numbers, and fine motor skills. And don't forget to read the book by Dr. Seuss for literacy practice!
