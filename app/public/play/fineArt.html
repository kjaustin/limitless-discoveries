Paint on Foil

paint /pānt/: a colored substance that is spread over a surface and dries to leave a thin decorative or protective coating

1. Gather Materials

Foil, A glue gun, Acrylic Paint, Paintbrush

2. Paint with Glue

Design the edge of the shapes using a hot glue gun on the foil. This may need to be done by the adult or with adult supervision.

3. Paint on Foil

Once the glue has dried allow the child to paint the shapes on the foil however she pleases.

Marbled Paper

marble /ˈmärbəl/: stain or streak (something) so that it looks like variegated marble

1. Gather Materials

Shaving Foam, Shallow baking dish, Liquid watercolor paint (or watered-down food coloring), Stir stick, Card stock, Scraper (piece of cardboard)

2. Spray baking dish with shaving foam.
3. Use a dropper to add drops of liquid watercolor paint on top of the shaving cream and swirl the paint around.

You can use watered down food coloring, liquid/craft acrylics, and tempera paint as well.

4. Press a piece of cardstock into the paint, lift up, and scrape up the excess shaving cream.

Rock Painting

rock /räk/: the solid mineral material forming part of the surface of the earth and other similar planets, exposed on the surface or underlying the soil or oceans

1. Prep the paint.

To prep your paint for marbling, mix together one part Martha Stewart Acrylic Paint and 2 parts Martha Stewart Marbling Medium in small cups or on a mixing palette using craft sticks or plastic spoons.

2. Use the tilting marbling method.

Drip prepped paint onto rock using a craft stick or plastic spoon. Tilt rock in different directions to get paint to run and swirl together. If paint is not runny enough, dilute with more marbling medium. Set aside to dry on cooling rack or craft plate. Once dry, seal with clear acrylic spray sealer if rock will be placed outside.
