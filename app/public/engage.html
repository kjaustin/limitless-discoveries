The Problem with Modern Schooling

As parents we are faced with an enormous amount of responsibility, in which our time, patience, faith, and empathy are tested day by day. Education is only a fraction of what we and others desire in children, and yet there is no one way to educate children, let alone any one individual.

It struck me that everyone was just sort of doing stuff because someone had done it before them. And that maybe our teachers & those creating our curriculum did not, in fact, ever stop to think about what the point of it all was. To question it.



Why was education focused on learning by rote? Of memorising a specific list of things to then just write down in a test at the end of the year? Of not so much learning stuff, but, rather, just memorising stuff.

Henry Latham

How do we provide our children with a strong educational foundation to have the ability to succeed in any pursuit imaginable? How do we instill a passion to learn independently while still focusing on the materials and subjects important to us?

How do we do this with patience, kindness, respect, and most of all love?

Revive Creativity, Confidence, and Curiousity
