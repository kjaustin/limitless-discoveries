Traditional Textbook

  • PRINCIPLES
  • SCHEDULE
  • MATERIALS
  • RESOURCES


The textbook is a book used as a standard source of information for formal study of a subject and an instrument for teaching and learning.

If textbooks follow a sequence, as within a series, it provides consistency between levels.

The Challenges and Benefits

PROS
  • It provides a syllabus for the course because the authors of the syllabus have made decisions about what will be learned and in what order.

  • It provides security for the students because they have a kind of a road map of the course: they know what to expect and they know what is expected from them.

  • It provides a set of visuals, activities, readings, etc., and so saves the teacher time in finding or developing such materials.

  • It provides teachers with a basis for assessing students’ learning. Some textbooks include tests or evaluation tools.

  • It may include supporting materials (teacher’s guide, cd, worksheets, and video.)

  • It provides consistency within a program across a given level, if all teachers use the same textbook.
CHALLENGES
  • The textbook/workbook approach doesn’t take into consideration your children’s individual learning style or their interests.

  • This approach assumes there is a set amount of information that constitutes an education and this info can be broken down into daily lessons.

  • This approach can be expensive when teaching multiple children of different ages.

  • It doesn’t encourage original, independent thinking as much as non-traditional approaches.

The textbook approach provides confidence and security for an inexperienced teacher who finds adapting existing textbooks challenging especially for tailored workrelated courses.

Traditional Textbook Principles

1. A STANDARD SOURCE OF INFORMATION

The Traditional Textbook approach is a framework that allows the learner to organize their learning.

2. LEARNING ASSESSMENT

Learning is assessed multiple times a year to ensure both student and teacher are meeting important goals.

3. A RESOURCE OF INSPIRATION

Books are the best way to learn and gain inspiration even in a traditional textbook format.

4. DEEP COMPREHENSION OF ONE SUBJECT

A textbook provides all knowledge and expertise on any given subject specifically tailored to the child's level.

For the learners the textbook is one of the most important sources of contact they have with the language. It is a framework or guide that helps them to organize their learning.
