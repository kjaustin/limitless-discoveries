The Challenges and Benefits

PROS
  • Short, interesting lessons will keep both the homeschooling parent and his or her children happy.

  • Living Books are never boring

  • Emphasis on experience rather than dry texts; the hands-on method usually leads to better retention of what is learned.

  • The Charlotte Mason Method allows for a lot of creativity.

  • This methodology could be heaven for young artists, aspiring writers and linguists.
CHALLENGES
  • Costs: Good books can be quite expensive.

  • Daily walks in the sunshine are nice, but might be a lot less fun in heavy rain or cold, stormy weather.

  • Some people may feel overwhelmed at the whole idea of using a curriculum consisting of Living Books and are uncertain as to whether or not their choice of books would be the right one.

The approach encourages spending time outdoors and learning the living ways of nature.

Living Literature Principles

1. FORMATION OF HABITS

The Charlotte Mason approach focuses on developing good habits into the life of a child as they grow.

2. SHORT LESSONS

To keep your child engaged and fully attentive, Charlotte Mason lessons remain very short. As your child grows older the lesson times increase.

3. LIVING BOOKS

Living books is the Charlotte Mason curriculum. These living books focus on first hand experience, autobiographies, biographies, classical writings, scriptures, and poetry.

4. NARRATION

Children naturally narrate things about their day and take this into their education. Oral narration is taught when a child reaches about the age of ten.

5. COPYWORK

The Charlotte Mason approach utilizes copying living books in order to allow your child good thoughts to meditate and foster ideas.

6. DICTATION

Dictation is a form of grammar lessons that effectively teaches spelling, sentence formation, and rules of grammar.

7. NATURE STUDIES

Through the approach children are involved in the nature around them, learning from creatures they observe, and getting fresh air and exercise daily.

8. PICTURE STUDY

Students study a masterpiece, retell the picture in their own words and select their favorite for their own attempt to create a replica.

9. MUSIC APPRECIATION

Students study a composer's music and life. This study of the arts allows for a balanced education and well rounded child.

Handwriting and spelling is taught by using passages from great books that communicate great ideas rather than using just a list of words.
