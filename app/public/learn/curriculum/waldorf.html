Waldorf education emphasizes the principle "to produce individuals who are able, in and of themselves, to impart meaning to their lives". The curriculum is as broad as time will allow, and balances academics subjects with artistic and practical activities..

Academics are de-emphasized in the early years of schooling to focus on the arts, music, and foreign languages.

The Challenges and Benefits

PROS
  • The Waldorf approach recognizes the essential divinity within each human being and seeks to instill within the child a sense of wonder and reverence, through legends, fairytales, myths, art, dance, and music.
  • The approach believes education is most effective when it approaches the child through the attributes of each developmental stage.
  • It emphasizes the developmental stages of children, reaffirms the role of the teacher, and integrates the arts into every aspect of the curriculum.
  • It immerses the child in a rich musical and artistic tapestry that provides ample opportunity to develop all aspects of the child.
CHALLENGES
  • The Waldorf approach utilizes Christian teachings to provide inspiration and moral guidance through the medium of storytelling. This may be a plus for individuals seeking a specific religious curriculum, however it does limit cultural religious adaptations.
  • The approach focuses on eliminating early education and directs children to wait to learn to read until the age of 7 or 8. Due to our current culture of print, the impulse to read may come from the natural curiosity and awareness of the child at a much earlier age.

Waldorf schooling is to educate the whole child, "head, heart and hands"

Waldorf Principles

1. ATTENTIVENESS TO THE INDIVIDUAL CHILD

"We should not learn to teach out of any book other than the one lying open before us and consisting of the children themselves."

2. STORYTELLING

â€œIf you want your children to be intelligent, read them fairy tales. If you want them to be more intelligent, read them more fairy tales."

3. CONNECT WITH NATURE

Connecting with nature means teaching our children to be more attentive to the world around them.

4. FREE TO PLAY

Waldorf education is based on the principle that the simplest toys foster the greatest creativity.

5. ESTABLISHED ROUTINES

Rituals and routines give children a sense of security and provide them with roots.

6. THE IMPORTANCE OF THE FINE ARTS

Children are provided with unstructured moments in which they can practice creative play through the fine arts.

By freely using arts and activities in the service of teaching academics, an internal motivation to learn is developed in the students, doing away with the need for competitive testing and grading.
