Montessori is a method of education that is based on self-directed activity, hands-on learning and collaborative play. Dr. Maria Montessori discovered that experiential learning in this type of classroom led to a deeper understanding of language, mathematics, science, music, social interactions and much more.

The Montessori approach fosters peer-to-peer learning through a mixed-age environment.

The Challenges and Benefits

PROS
  • Children work, develop, and learn at their own individual pace through hands-on learning materials.

  • Children tend to be more able to manage themselves and think independently. This in turn is better for developing an entrepreneurial skill set.

  • Mixed aged groups allow for children to learn from one another, teach one another and develop life skills such as inclusion and acceptance.

  • The Montessori approach strives to encourage a cultivated love for learning.
CHALLENGES
  • It's expensive due to the many durable and high-quality materials, as well as the lengthy and in-depth training.

  • Montessori is a much looser curriculum which can create room for some subjects to fall by the wayside.

  • Because Montessori is strong in fostering a sense of independence, it makes it difficult for students to collaborate in teams and work under a rigid authority in future employment situations.

  • Montessori classrooms are built to allow movement and change and the teachers tend to guide more than directly instruct. This could be intimidating to those that like routine and structure.

This approach allows for children to work, develop, and learn at their own individual pace.

Montessori Principles

1. MOVEMENT & COGNITION

Movement and cognition are closely entwined, and movement can enhance thinking and learning.

2. CHOICE

Learning and well-being are improved when people have a sense of control over their lives.

3. INTEREST

People learn better when they are interested in what they are learning.

4. AVOID EXTRINSIC REWARDS

Tying extrinsic rewards to an activity, like money for reading or high grades for tests, negatively impacts motivation to engage in that activity when the reward is withdrawn.

5. LEARNING FROM & WITH PEERS

Collaborative arrangements can be very conducive to learning.

6. CONTEXTUAL LEARNING

Learning situated in meaningful contexts is often deeper and richer than learning in abstract contexts.

7. ORDER IN ENVIRONMENT & MIND

Order in the environment is beneficial to children.

Montessori is all about creating a beautiful and accessible environment designed to meet the needs of children in a specific age range.

Weekly Montessori Schedule

MONDAY

Day 1

  • PRACTICAL LIFE // Pegs and Basket, Pom Poms
  • SENSORY // Pink Tower, Brown Stairs
  • LANGUAGE // Simple Nomenclature Cards
  • MATH // Number Rods
  • CULTURE // Introduction to Seasons

TUESDAY

Day 2

  • PRACTICAL LIFE // Transferring with a Spoon, Marbles and Tongs
  • SENSORY // The Cylinder Blocks
  • LANGUAGE // Logical Puzzles
  • MATH // Introduce Sand Paper and Numbers
  • CULTURE // Time Line of the Day of a Child

WEDNESDAY

Day 3

  • PRACTICAL LIFE // Dry Pouring & Sorting
  • SENSORY // Red Rods
  • LANGUAGE // Rhyming Pictures
  • MATH // Spindle Boxes
  • CULTURE // Weather Calendar

THURSDAY

Day 4

  • PRACTICAL LIFE // Bottles & Lids, Dusting & Sweeping

  • LANGUAGE // Opposites

Day 5

  • PRACTICAL LIFE // Pass a Ball
  • SENSORY // Constructive Triangles
  • LANGUAGE // Metal Insects
  • MATH // Short Bead Stairs
  • CULTURE // Growth of a Child Sequence
